## 1. 🔍 Identify Their "Gauge Comfort Zone"

In knitting, **Gauge (GG)** is everything. It defines the density and thickness of the stitch.
*   **❄️ Low Gauge (e.g., 3GG, 5GG, 7GG)**: Chunky, cozy, heavy winter knits.
*   **🌸 High Gauge (e.g., 14GG, 16GG, 18GG)**: Sleek, lightweight, fine-gauge spring layers.

**The Insider Rule**: Most specialized factories have a "sweet spot." A factory that masters ultra-fine 18GG silk-cashmere may struggle with the heavy tension and bulky yarn of a 3GG cardigan. 

> **💡 Pro Tip**: Don't settle for a "one-stop shop." Look for a specialist whose core machinery and expertise match your specific product category.

---

## 2. 💰 Respect the Sampling Cost

A common friction point for new brands is the price gap between samples and bulk production. If a sample costs $150 while the bulk unit is $25, that isn’t a markup—it’s **engineering**.

*   **💻 CAD Programming**: A senior technician spends hours on specialized software for a single garment.
*   **⚙️ Machine Downtime**: Production machines must be stopped and recalibrated specifically for your test swatch.
*   **🧵 Yarn Sourcing**: Factories often buy single cones of yarn at retail prices for samples.

**⚠️ Red Flag**: If a factory offers "free" or suspiciously cheap samples, they are likely skipping the critical engineering phase, which leads to sizing disasters during bulk production.

---

## 3. ♻️ Virgin vs. Recycled Cashmere: The Performance Gap

Sustainability is a powerful brand promise, but you must understand the material trade-offs:

| Material | Handfeel | Pilling Resistance | Stability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Virgin Cashmere** | ✨ Superior softness | ✅ High (Long fibers) | 🛡️ Very Stable |
| **Recycled Cashmere** | 🧶 Slightly coarser | ⚠️ Moderate (Short fibers) | 📉 Variable |

**📊 Transparency Check**: A top-tier factory won't just follow the trend. They will provide honest shrinkage and pilling data for both options to help you decide based on your brand’s lifespan promise.

---

## 4. 🔍 The "Inside-Out" Quality Audit

The true craft of a sweater isn't on the front—it’s in the **Linking** (the seam where panels are joined).

**When visiting a factory, check the linking section for these defects:**
1.  **❌ Skipped Stitches**: Weak spots that will eventually unravel.
2.  **🕳️ Dropped Stitches**: Small holes at the edge of the seams.
3.  **🎗️ Tension Issues**: Seams that pucker or feel stiff against the skin.

> **🏆 Quality Benchmark**: Ask for their internal linking defect rate. A professional facility should maintain a rate of **<2%**.

## 5. 🧪 Shrinkage: Don’t Trust, Verify

Knitwear shrinkage is physics, but it must be controlled. A professional factory compensates for this during the pattern-making stage.

**The Standard Formula:**
$$Shrinkage = \frac{Pre\_wash\ size - Post\_wash\ size}{Pre\_wash\ size} \times 100\%$$

**The Verification Filter**:
Ask every potential supplier: *"How do you ensure bulk shrinkage matches your sample shrinkage?"* 
The best partners will show you a written **🧼 wash-test protocol** and a report for every specific yarn lot they receive.
